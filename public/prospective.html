Prospective Members

Welcome! This page is our comprehensive resource for students interested in joining Badgerloop. Below are answers to some of the most common questions we get from prospective members.

What is Badgerloop?

Badgerloop is a student organization at UW-Madison that competes in the SpaceX Hyperloop Pod Competition. This Competition is open to universities from around the world; we have consistently placed as a top-two hyperloop team in the United States.

What is Hyperloop?

Hyperloop is a futuristic form of transportation invented by Elon Musk. It involves putting a pod of passengers in a depressurized tube; the end product is both a fast and sustainable form of transportation. Typically, we build a prototype hyperloop pod every year and test it at SpaceX’s headquarters in Hawthorne, CA. Given the uncertainty surrounding COVID-19, we cannot be sure what the state of the annual competition is, but at a minimum, we want to do two things this year:

  • Build a test track with a well-defined curve at one of our sponsor's facilities
  • Design and build a new hyperloop pod that is able to handle said curve

About the Team

Joining Badgerloop affords a unique, hands-on learning experience. Building a hyperloop pod offers a one-of-a-kind chance to apply what you learn in classes to a real-life problem. Anyone can join, regardless of major, and you don’t need any prior knowledge or experience to get involved. Our Team Leads are very approachable and always willing to help new members learn the necessary skills and programs. General members typically spend about 4 hours a week working on different projects. Additionally, we meet as an organization once every other week.


  • Electrical

      Summary

      The Electrical team is responsible for designing and integrating the pod's on-board hardware, high voltage battery pack, power-train, embedded firmware, and external dashboard. Common majors represented in our Electrical team are Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering and Computer Science!



      Battery Responsible for providing high voltage, high current power to the pod for propulsion in a safe manner.

      Controls Develops and maintains all the software that runs on the pod itself.

      Low Voltage Responsible for the pod's avionic systems. This involves working with circuit board design and bring-up, electrical harnessing, sensors, and other tasks that integrate the mechanical systems with the electronic controls that guide our run in the tube.

      Electrical Reliability Spreads out some of the electrical oriented responsibilities and tasks that will help the entire electrical and mechanical systems succeed. This includes (but is not limited to) quality control, electrical-mechanical system integration, and system testing for electrical hardware.

      Software Handles everything to do with the pod dashboard, and other software orientated services we may be interested in setting up, such as the bill of materials and automated testing.

      Powertrain Responsible for integrating the motor controller and motor into the electrical system of the pod.

  • Mechanical

      Summary

      The Mechanical team has the responsibility of designing and manufacturing a structurally sound, functional, and optimized pod to the best of our abilities. Common majors represented in our Mechanical Team are Mechanical Engineering and Engineering Mechanics and Astronautics.



      Analysis Ensures the analysis on all components is done correctly and well documented. Includes hand calculations and FEA's (Finite Element Analysis)

      Braking Designs mechanisms and components required to safely stop the pod at the end of a run.

      Mechanical Reliability Works with the electrical team to make sure integration with electrical systems will go smoothly and designs are sound. Additionally, this team is responsible for general mechanical tasks that support the overall team.

      Propulsion Provides means of accelerating the pod to maximum speed possible.

      Stability Constrains the pod's degrees of freedom to provide a smooth, controlled ride by means of a suspension system.

      Structural Designs and fabricates the chassis, shell, and pressure vessel (if necessary).

  • Operations

      Summary

      The Operations team helps run our organization in a manner similar to that of a small business. Operations members represent a wide number of majors from the University including business, communications, finance and many more!



      Communications Runs and creates content for our social media channels. Also designs and coordinates organization apparel.

      Feasibility Analyzes research and designs studies to test the feasibility of hyperloop technology.

      Finance and Supply Chain Manages our budget and helps facilitate materials purchases.

      Industry Relations Creates and maintains relationships with companies in order to obtain funding.

      Outreach and Recruiting Facilitates events that spread the word about our organization to UW students and the Madison community.

      Virtual Reality Uses VR/AR systems to create new ways for people to interact with hyperloop technology.

      Website Handles everything related to www.badgerloop.org and helps set up any web-based services we’re interested in.
